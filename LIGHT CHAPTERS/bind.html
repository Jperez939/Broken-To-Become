Bind

How do aesthetics of repair may suggest both restoration and fragility?


Susie Campbell considers the emergence, within her own and others’ artistic practice, of an alternative approach: a practice of retaining damage, or at least making any repair visible.

Her work is primarily text-based but she also engages directly with fabric, making textile poetry which incorporates text.

She shows how, over the course of several projects, she has found a set of highly suggestive similarities or material kinships between text and textile on which to base a poetics as well as thematic concerns.

Visible holes, stains, darns and patches tend to appear across the surface of both her text and textile-based forms. She teases out what this might mean for an aesthetics of repair and considers whether all damage can or should be mended.

When it came to mending the tear, she found the repair itself created a hitch in the cloth, a scar that defaced and obscured the surface of both text and textile. The repair works up to a point. It recreates a ‘page’ on which some of the text can still be read and the function of the garment is returned to plausibility.

Susie quotes Kate Siklosi:

“Fragility itself is a thread, a fragile connection, between those things deemed breakable”
